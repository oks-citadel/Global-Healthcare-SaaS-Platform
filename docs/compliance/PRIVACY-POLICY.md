## Definitions

- **Protected Health Information (PHI):** Any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to a specific individual.
- **Personal Data:** Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
- **Covered Entity:** A health plan, healthcare clearinghouse, or healthcare provider that transmits health information electronically.
- **Business Associate:** A person or entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of, or provides services to, a covered entity that involves PHI.

## Your Privacy Rights

### HIPAA Privacy Rights

Under HIPAA, you have the following rights:

#### 1. Right to Access Your Medical Records

You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your medical records.

- **How to Request:** Submit a written request to our Privacy Officer
- **Response Time:** Within 30 days (may extend to 60 days)
- **Format:** Electronic or paper copy
- **Fees:** We may charge a reasonable cost-based fee for copies

#### 2. Right to Amend Your Medical Records

You have the right to request corrections to your medical records if you believe they are incorrect or incomplete.

- **How to Request:** Submit a written request with supporting documentation
- **Response Time:** Within 60 days
- **Note:** We may deny your request if the information is accurate and complete, but you can submit a statement of disagreement

#### 3. Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You have the right to receive a list of certain disclosures of your PHI made in the past 6 years.

- **How to Request:** Submit a written request
- **Free Requests:** First request in a 12-month period is free
- **Fees:** Reasonable cost-based fee for additional requests

#### 4. Right to Request Restrictions

You have the right to request restrictions on how we use or disclose your PHI.

- **Note:** We are not required to agree to your request, except in specific circumstances (e.g., disclosure to health plan for services you paid for out-of-pocket)
- **How to Request:** Submit a written request explaining the restriction you want

#### 5. Right to Request Confidential Communications

You have the right to request that we communicate with you in a specific way or at a specific location.

- **Example:** "Please contact me at my work phone, not my home phone"
- **How to Request:** Submit a written request

#### 6. Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice

You have the right to receive a paper copy of this Privacy Policy at any time.

- **How to Request:** Contact our Privacy Officer or download from our website

#### 7. Right to Revoke Authorization

You have the right to revoke any authorization you have given us to use or disclose your PHI (except to the extent we have already acted on your authorization).

- **How to Request:** Submit a written revocation

### GDPR Rights (for EU Residents)

If you are a resident of the European Union, you have additional rights under GDPR:

#### 1. Right to Data Portability

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and to transmit it to another controller.

#### 2. Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data under certain circumstances.

**Note:** We may be required to retain certain health information for legal or regulatory reasons.

#### 3. Right to Restrict Processing

You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data under certain circumstances.

#### 4. Right to Object

You have the right to object to processing of your personal data based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.

#### 5. Right to Withdraw Consent

You have the right to withdraw consent at any time (where processing is based on consent).

#### 6. Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority if you believe your data protection rights have been violated.

**EU Supervisory Authority:** Your local data protection authority

## Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your PHI:

### Technical Safeguards

- **Encryption at Rest:** AES-256-GCM encryption for all stored PHI
- **Encryption in Transit:** TLS 1.3 for all data transmissions
- **Access Controls:** Role-based access control (RBAC)
- **Authentication:** Multi-factor authentication (MFA) available
- **Audit Logging:** Comprehensive logging of all PHI access
- **Intrusion Detection:** Real-time monitoring for security threats
- **Vulnerability Scanning:** Regular security assessments
- **Secure Backups:** Encrypted, geo-redundant backups

### Administrative Safeguards

- **Security Policies:** Comprehensive information security policies
- **Workforce Training:** Regular HIPAA and security training
- **Access Management:** Least-privilege access principles
- **Incident Response:** Documented incident response procedures
- **Risk Assessments:** Annual security risk assessments
- **Business Associate Agreements:** HIPAA-compliant contracts with all vendors

### Physical Safeguards

- **Data Center Security:** SOC 2 Type II certified facilities (Azure)
- **Workstation Security:** Screen locks, encryption, physical security
- **Device Controls:** Secure disposal and sanitization procedures

### Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach affecting your PHI, we will:

1. **Investigate** the breach within 24 hours of discovery
2. **Notify You** within 60 days (as required by HIPAA)
3. **Notify Authorities** (HHS and applicable data protection authorities)
4. **Provide Information** about the breach and steps you can take
5. **Take Corrective Action** to prevent future breaches

## Data Retention

### Retention Periods

We retain your information for the following periods:

- **Medical Records:** 10 years from last date of service (or longer if required by state law)
- **Audit Logs:** 6 years (HIPAA requirement)
- **Billing Records:** 7 years (IRS requirement)
- **Consent Records:** 6 years from date of revocation
- **Account Information:** Duration of account plus 1 year
- **De-identified Data:** Indefinitely (for research and analytics)

### Secure Deletion

After the retention period expires, we securely delete your information using:

- **Multi-pass data overwriting** (DoD 5220.22-M standard)
- **Cryptographic erasure** (for encrypted data)
- **Physical destruction** (for hardware)
- **Verification and certification** of deletion

### Legal Holds

We may retain information beyond the standard retention period if:

- Required by law or regulation
- Subject to litigation hold
- Part of an ongoing investigation
- Necessary for legal claims
