## 5. REQUESTING ACCESS TO RECORDS

### 5.1 Who Can Request Access

**Personal Requesters:**
- Data subjects requesting their own personal information
- Authorized representatives with written authority

**Other Requesters:**
- Any person requiring records for the exercise or protection of rights

### 5.2 Request Process

**Step 1: Complete Request Form**
- Use Form A (request by personal requester) or Form B (request by other requester)
- Forms available at www.thetheunifiedhealth.com/paia or from our offices

**Step 2: Submit Request**
Submit to:
- **Email:** paia.requests@thetheunifiedhealth.com
- **Post:** Information Officer, [POSTAL ADDRESS]
- **Physical Delivery:** [PHYSICAL ADDRESS]

**Step 3: Pay Prescribed Fee** (if applicable)
- Request fee: R50.00
- Access fees: As per PAIA regulations
- Payment details provided upon request

**Step 4: Await Response**
- Acknowledgment within 7 days
- Decision within 30 days of receipt

### 5.3 Required Information

Your request must include:
- Full name and contact details
- Identity or registration number
- Description of records requested
- Form in which access is required
- Postal or street address, fax number, or email
- Right being exercised or protected (if applicable)
- Proof of identity
- Proof of authority (if acting on behalf of another)

### 5.4 Fees

**Request Fee:** R50.00 (except personal requesters)

**Access Fees** (per PAIA regulations):
- Copies: R1.10 per page
- Printed copies: As per actual cost
- Computer-readable format: R75.00 + R7.50 per page
- Transcription: R24.00 per hour or part thereof
- Copy of visual images: Actual cost
- Search and preparation: R30.00 per hour (max 6 hours)

### 5.5 Response Times

- **Acknowledgment:** Within 7 days
- **Decision:** Within 30 days
- **Extension:** Additional 30 days (with notice and reasons)

## 6. GROUNDS FOR REFUSAL

Access may be refused if:
- Records do not exist or cannot be found
- Request is manifestly frivolous or vexatious
- Substantial and unreasonable diversion of resources
- Disclosure would involve unreasonable breach of privacy
- Commercial information would be disclosed
- Records are legally privileged
- Research information would be compromised
- Third party information without consent
- Safety or health of individuals would be endangered
